#Project overview

Bright Motors has recently appointed a new Head of Sales whose mission is to expand the dealership network, improve sales performance, and optimise inventory. 

As a Junior Data Analyst, this project involved extracting actionable insights from historical car sales data and preparing a presentation to guide future sales and marketing strategies.

The dataset — Bright Car Sales — captures daily transactional and pricing information for vehicles sold by Bright Motors across multiple US states.

#Objective

This analysis set out to answer the following key business questions:

Which car makes and models generate the most revenue?

What is the relationship between price, mileage, and year of manufacture?

Which regions or states have the highest sales volumes?

What are the emerging trends in customer purchasing preferences?

What recommendations can increase dealership profitability and efficiency?

#Key findings
1.Budget cars dominate — accounting for the majority of revenue, confirming affordability as the key customer priority

2.Nissan and Chevrolet are the top performing makes by total revenue

3.The F-150 is the single highest revenue generating model

4.Low mileage vehicles command significantly higher selling prices

5.Regional performance varies widely — top states present clear opportunities for dealership expansion

#Recommendations

1.Prioritise top makes and models in inventory — Ford and Chevrolet consistently drive the most revenue and should remain core stock

2.Expand dealership presence in high revenue states — focus resources on top performing regions while developing strategies for weaker markets

3.Stock more low mileage vehicles — data shows these command higher prices and can increase average revenue per sale

4.Focus on high margin makes — shifting the inventory mix toward higher margin vehicles could significantly improve overall profitability
